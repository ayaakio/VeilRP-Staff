1.0 Ticket Handling Guide

1.1 Purpose of Tickets

Tickets are used so players can contact staff for help, questions, issues, or server-related concerns.

Staff should treat tickets as a support system first, making sure responses are clear, helpful, and professional.

  • Respond in a calm and respectful manner.
  • Read the ticket fully before answering.
  • Make sure you understand what the player is asking before responding.
  • Keep the conversation organised and on topic.

1.2 First Response

When entering a ticket, staff should acknowledge it properly and begin by understanding the issue.

  • Greet the user politely.
  • Ask for more context if the ticket is unclear.
  • Do not rush into an answer without understanding the problem.
  • Keep replies simple, direct, and easy to follow.

The first response sets the tone for the rest of the ticket, so it should always be professional and helpful.

1.3 General Questions

If a ticket is a general question, staff should answer and support to the best of their ability.

  • Give the most accurate answer you can.
  • If you are unsure, ask another staff member rather than guessing.
  • Do not leave general support tickets unanswered if they can be handled by staff.
  • Make sure the player leaves with a clear answer or direction.

1.4 Tagging the Relevant Parties

Not every ticket should be handled by the same person. Staff should tag the relevant people depending on what the ticket is about.

  • If it is an owner matter, tag the owners.
  • If it is a dev matter, tag the developers.
  • If it is a general support matter, staff should handle it where possible.
  • If a matter clearly belongs to another team, do not try to force an answer yourself.

Tagging the correct people helps tickets get handled faster and more accurately.

1.5 Owner Matters

Some tickets require owner review rather than normal staff handling.

  • Tag the owners for tickets involving high-level decisions, sensitive issues, or matters outside normal staff responsibility.
  • Do not attempt to answer owner-level matters as if they are routine support questions.
  • If you are unsure whether something should go to owners, ask internally before giving the player a firm answer.

1.6 Developer Matters

Tickets involving technical issues, scripts, bugs, or development changes should be directed to the developers.

  • Tag the devs if the ticket is clearly about a script issue, bug, panel issue, or server-side development problem.
  • Do not promise fixes or timeframes unless confirmed by the dev team.
  • Gather useful details first where possible, such as screenshots, clips, error messages, or clear descriptions.

1.7 Staff Responsibility

Staff are expected to support tickets properly rather than avoiding them or passing off simple issues unnecessarily.

  • Handle what you reasonably can.
  • Do not tag owners or devs for simple questions that staff can answer themselves.
  • Use judgment when deciding whether to answer, escalate, or tag someone else.
  • If you cannot help directly, at least move the ticket in the right direction.

1.8 Tone and Professionalism

How staff speak in tickets matters just as much as the answer itself.

  • Be respectful and professional at all times.
  • Do not argue with players in tickets.
  • Do not be dismissive, rude, or sarcastic.
  • Even if a player is frustrated, keep your own tone controlled.

2.0 Good Practice

2.1 Best Practice for Tickets

  • Read the full ticket before replying.
  • Ask questions if the issue is unclear.
  • Tag the correct people when needed.
  • Handle general questions where staff are able to.
  • Keep responses clear, polite, and organised.
  • Make sure the player understands the outcome before closing the ticket.

2.2 Closing Tickets

Tickets should only be closed once the issue has been answered, resolved, or properly redirected.

  • Confirm the player has been given an answer or next step.
  • Do not close tickets abruptly without explanation.
  • If the matter has been passed to owners or devs, make that clear in the ticket.
  • Leave the ticket in a state that another staff member can understand if they need to review it later.

3.0 What to Avoid

3.1 Poor Ticket Handling

  • Do not ignore tickets that staff could answer.
  • Do not tag owners or devs for every minor issue.
  • Do not guess answers when you are unsure.
  • Do not promise outcomes you cannot guarantee.
  • Do not be rude, dismissive, or impatient with players.
  • Do not close tickets before they are properly dealt with.
